Eligibility for Voter ID

  • Indian citizen by birth or naturalization
  • Minimum age 18 years (as of registration date)
  • Resident of the constituency for at least 3 months
  • Not disqualified by law (mental illness, criminal conviction)
  • Not a citizen of any foreign country
  • Not already registered in another constituency
  • Must be a permanent resident of India

Documents Required

  • Proof of Identity (Aadhaar, Passport, Driving License, PAN)
  • Proof of Address (Utility bill, Bank statement, Lease agreement)
  • Proof of Citizenship (Birth certificate, School certificate)
  • Date of Birth Proof (Birth certificate, School records)
  • Passport Size Photograph (3.5 x 4.5 cm, color)
  • Completed Form 6 (New registration)
  • Completed Form 8 (Correction/updation)
  • Resident Certificate (optional, for address proof)

Types of Voter Registration

Regular Voter

Standard registration in home constituency

Non-Resident Indian (NRI)

Indian citizen living abroad temporarily

PIO (Person of Indian Origin)

Foreign nationals of Indian descent

Displaced Person

Registered from relief camp or temporary shelter

Service Voter

Armed forces personnel posted elsewhere

Migrant Worker

Temporarily residing in another state

Steps to Register as Voter

1

Check Eligibility

Verify you meet all requirements: Indian citizen, 18+ years, resident for 3+ months in constituency.

Online Check: Visit ECI Website

2

Download Form 6

Download the voter registration form (Form 6) from your state election commission website or get it from booth office.

Online Download: Download Form 6 (ECI Portal)

3

Fill Application Form

Complete Form 6 with your personal details, address, date of birth, and citizenship information accurately.

4

Attach Documents

Attach self-attested copies of identity proof, address proof, age proof, and a recent passport-size photograph.

5

Submit Application

Submit the form at your nearest booth office or election commission office. Keep a receipt for reference.

Online Submission: Apply Online (NVSP Portal)

6

Field Verification

Election officials verify your residential address and authenticity of submitted documents through field visit.

7

Objection Period

Application is published in the electoral roll. Anyone can file objections within 30 days if they have valid reasons.

8

Hear Objections

If objections are filed, hearing is conducted. You can present your case if required for final decision.

9

Final Approval

After objection period, election commission approves registration and your name is added to electoral roll.

10

EPIC Card Issuance

Voter ID (EPIC) card is printed and issued. You can vote in elections once registered. Card sent by post or collect from office.

Track Status: Check Status (NVSP Portal)

Important Notes

  • Voter ID (EPIC) is a lifetime document. You can vote for life once registered
  • Must update voter details within 30 days of address change or other modifications
  • You can check electoral roll online on state election commission website
  • Voter ID is a valid government identity proof, accepted in most institutions
  • EPIC card is issued free of cost - no fees or charges involved
  • You can get duplicate EPIC if lost or damaged from booth office
  • Registration can be cancelled only by statutory grounds (shifting out, disqualification)
  • Online registration on NVSP portal is fastest and most convenient method
  • Form 7 & 8 are used for amendments or removal from electoral roll
  • Polling booth location can be checked on day of election using booth finder app
