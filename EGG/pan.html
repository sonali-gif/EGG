Eligibility for PAN Card

  • Any Indian resident (citizen, NRI, HUF, AOP)
  • Individuals earning income in India
  • Self-employed professionals and businessmen
  • Minors (with guardian consent)
  • Foreign nationals with income in India
  • Non-resident Indians (NRI)
  • Trusts, partnership firms, companies
  • No age limit - from minors to senior citizens

Documents Required

  • Proof of Identity (Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, Driving License)
  • Proof of Address (Same documents as identity if they contain address)
  • Proof of Date of Birth (Birth Certificate, School Certificate)
  • Passport Size Photograph (4x6 cm, color or B&W)
  • Aadhaar Number (now mandatory for new PAN applications)
  • Email Address & Mobile Number
  • Income Proof (Salary slip, Bank statement, ITR)

Types of PAN Cards

Individual PAN

For salaried employees and self-employed individuals

Partnership Firm PAN

For businesses registered as partnership firms

Company PAN

For registered private and public companies

HUF (Hindu Undivided Family) PAN

For joint family property and income

Trust PAN

For registered charitable trusts and NGOs

AOP (Association of Persons) PAN

For groups of individuals engaged in business/profession

Steps to Obtain PAN Card

1

Apply Online

Visit NSDL or UTIITSL portal and fill online PAN application form. Available in Hindi and English.

Online Application: NSDL Portal

2

Fill Form 49A (New)

Fill application form with personal details, address, Aadhaar number, and email. Digital signature or scanned documents required.

3

Upload Documents

Upload scanned copies (PDF format) of identity proof, address proof, photograph, and other documents. File size limit 1 MB each.

Important Information

  • Processing Time: Online: 3-7 days (email); Offline: 7-10 days
  • Physical Card: Arrives within 21 days at registered address (via registered post)
  • Cost: ‚Çπ100 (Individual), ‚Çπ500-1000 (Business entities)
  • Validity: Lifelong validity. Once allotted, it never changes
  • Pan Number Format: 10 characters (5 letters + 4 digits + 1 letter)
  • Duplicate PAN: One Aadhaar number = One PAN only. Duplicate PAN is illegal
  • Mandatory Since: 2017 for certain financial transactions and property deals
  • Digital PAN: Can use PAN immediately after email confirmation; physical card just a copy

Alternatives & Tips

  • Digital PAN: Can be used immediately after email allotment; physical card is just paper copy
  • Why Important: Mandatory for opening bank accounts, filing income tax returns, high-value transactions
  • Link with Aadhaar: Link PAN with Aadhaar to avoid penalties and comply with tax laws
  • Update Address: Address in PAN can be updated online; doesn't change PAN number
  • Lost Card: Can get duplicate from NSDL portal; original PAN remains same
  • International Transactions: Required for remittances and foreign investments
  • Check PAN Status: Verify status on NSDL website using email/mobile and PAN details
