Eligibility for Driving License

  • Minimum age 18 years (16 years for non-transport vehicles in some states)
  • Must pass the learner's license test
  • Good eyesight and hearing
  • No mental or physical disabilities affecting driving
  • Indian citizen or foreigner with valid visa
  • Must own the vehicle registered in their name
  • No criminal record related to traffic violations

Documents Required

  • Proof of Identity (Aadhaar, Passport, PAN Card)
  • Proof of Address (Recent utility bill, Aadhaar)
  • Date of Birth Proof (Birth Certificate, School Certificate)
  • Passport Size Photograph (4x6 cm)
  • Medical Certificate (Form 5)
  • Vehicle Registration Certificate
  • Completed Application Form (Form 4/8)
  • Proof of Residence (Driving License, Aadhaar)

Types of Driving License

LMV (Light Motor Vehicle)

Cars, Jeeps, Vans up to 3,500 kg

HMV (Heavy Motor Vehicle)

Trucks, Buses, Heavy vehicles

MCWG (Motorcycle with Gears)

Two-wheelers above 50cc

MOPED

Two-wheelers up to 50cc

Transport

Commercial vehicle license

Learner's License

Temporary permit to learn driving

Steps to Obtain Driving License

1

Apply for Learner's License

Fill out Form 4 and submit at your nearest RTO with required documents. Appear for learner's test (written).

Online Option: Apply Online on Parivahan Portal

2

Pass Written Test

Answer 20 multiple-choice questions about traffic rules and road signs. Need 13/20 marks to pass.

3

Obtain Learner's License

Valid for 6 months. You can now practice driving with a licensed driver (minimum 18 years) in the car.

4

Practical Training

Practice driving for at least 40 hours. Learn vehicle handling, parking, and defensive driving techniques.

5

Schedule Practical Test

After 30 days from learner's license, book your practical driving test at the RTO with an appointment.

Online Booking: Book Slot on Parivahan Portal

6

Appear for Driving Test

Demonstrate driving skills: starting, stopping, turning, parking, lane discipline, and traffic rule compliance.

7

Medical Examination

Get a medical fitness certificate (Form 5) from a government-authorized medical practitioner. Check eyesight & hearing.

8

Submit Final Application

Submit Form 8 along with all documents, test results, and medical certificate to the RTO.

Online Submission: Submit via Parivahan Portal

9

Document Verification

RTO verifies all submitted documents and conducts a final check of your application details and records.

10

License Issuance

Your driving license will be issued within 2-7 working days. Collect from RTO or receive by post.

Track Status: Check Status on Parivahan Portal

Important Notes

  • Always carry your driving license when driving
  • Report any changes in address to RTO within 30 days
  • Renew your license before 30 days of expiry
  • Maintain traffic rules and regulations strictly
  • Update emergency contact details at RTO
  • Driving license is valid only for the categories mentioned
  • Different states may have varying procedures and fees
  • Online DL can be viewed through DigiLocker or Parivahan Portal
