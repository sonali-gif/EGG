PAN Card Guide

Eligibility for PAN Card

  • Indian citizens and residents
  • Individuals, HUFs, firms, companies
  • Trusts, associations, and other entities
  • Required for tax purposes
  • Mandatory for high-value transactions

Required Documents

Types of PAN Cards

Major PAN Card

For individuals aged 18 years and above. Used for tax filing, investments, and financial transactions.

Minor PAN Card

For individuals below 18 years. Can be applied by parents/guardians. Required for investments in minor's name.

Steps to Get PAN Card

1

Apply Online

Visit NSDL or UTIITSL website and fill Form 49A for new PAN or 49AA for NRI.

2

Upload Documents

Submit scanned copies of identity proof, address proof, and photograph.

3

Payment

Pay the application fee online (₹107 for Indian communication address).

4

Verification

Application processed and PAN number generated within 15-30 days.

5

Collect PAN Card

Download e-PAN or collect physical card from designated post office.

Important Information

Processing Time: 15-30 days for new PAN card

Cost: ₹107 for Indian address, ₹994 for foreign address

Validity: Lifetime validity, no renewal required

PAN Number: 10-character alphanumeric unique identifier

Uses: Income tax filing, investments, bank accounts, property transactions

Correction: Changes can be made online through NSDL portal

e-PAN: Digital version available instantly after approval
